About

SAHARA is an NGO in India directly benefitting over 15,00,000 children and their families every year, through more than 400 live welfare projects on education, healthcare, livelihood and women empowerment, in over 2000 remote villages and slums across 25 states of India. Educate the poor has continued to evolve since its beginnings in 1958, adapting to the specific needs of the countries in which it works. Originally conceived as a bond of friendship between the peoples of Southeast Asia, Children of the Mekong strived to stay faithful to its principal goal: to love and support poor, suffering children by offering them a future through education.
